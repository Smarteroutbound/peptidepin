## Complete Peptide User Journey Research (March 2026)

---

### PHASE 1: RESEARCH & PURCHASE

**How users decide what peptide to use:**
- Reddit communities (r/peptides, r/Tirzepatide, r/semaglutide) are the primary discovery channel
- Users research based on goal: weight loss (semaglutide, tirzepatide, retatrutide), healing (BPC-157, TB-500), anti-aging/GH (CJC-1295, Ipamorelin), or muscle/performance
- Protocol information comes from Reddit posts, YouTube videos, clinic websites, and vendor guides
- Many users start with a telehealth clinic prescription (especially GLP-1s) then move to research peptides for cost savings

**Where they buy:**
- Research peptide vendors (Biotech Peptides, Core Peptides, Limitless Biotech, etc.)
- Compounding pharmacies (for prescribed semaglutide/tirzepatide)
- All sold as "research use only" with certificates of analysis (purity reports)
- Prices vary: BPC-157 5mg ~$25-50, Semaglutide 5mg ~$40-80, Tirzepatide 10-30mg ~$50-150

**What arrives in the mail:**
- Lyophilized (freeze-dried) powder in small glass vials (typically 2-3mL borosilicate glass)
- Powder appears as a white to off-white puck, cake, or fluffy powder at bottom of vial
- Common vial sizes: 1mg, 2mg, 5mg, 10mg, 15mg, 30mg of peptide content
- Certificate of Analysis (COA) with purity percentage
- Separately purchased: bacteriostatic water (BAC water, 10mL or 30mL vials), insulin syringes, alcohol swabs
- BAC water contains 0.9% benzyl alcohol to inhibit bacterial growth

### PHASE 2: RECONSTITUTION (The Critical First Step)

**Exact step-by-step process:**

1. **Prep (5-10 min):** Allow peptide vial and BAC water to reach room temperature (30 min from fridge/freezer). Clean workspace. Wash hands. Have alcohol swabs ready.
2. **Inspect vial:** Powder should be dry, loosely packed, white to off-white. Discard if discolored, wet, or clumpy.
3. **Swab tops:** Wipe rubber stoppers of both vials with alcohol swabs. Let dry.
4. **Draw BAC water:** Using a fresh insulin syringe, draw the desired amount of BAC water from its vial.
5. **Inject into peptide vial:** Insert needle through rubber stopper. AIM AT THE VIAL WALL, not directly onto the powder cake. Let water trickle slowly down the glass wall. NEVER spray directly onto powder.
6. **Dissolve gently:** Do NOT shake. Gently swirl or roll the vial between fingers ~10 times. If not fully dissolved, let sit 15-30 minutes and swirl again.
7. **Verify dissolution:** Solution should be perfectly clear, no visible particles. Cloudiness = do not use.
8. **Label the vial:** Write date of reconstitution + amount of BAC water added + peptide name. This is critical for dosing math.
9. **Store immediately:** Refrigerate at 2-8C (36-46F). Keep away from light.

**How users decide how much BAC water to add:**

This is one of the BIGGEST confusion points. The amount of BAC water determines the concentration, which determines injection volume per dose. Users want to balance:
- Small enough volume per injection (less pain, easier to measure)
- Large enough that dose markings on syringe are readable
- Enough doses per vial before expiration (~28-30 days)

**Common BAC water amounts by peptide type:**

| Peptide | Vial Size | BAC Water | Concentration | Rationale |
|---------|-----------|-----------|---------------|-----------|
| BPC-157 | 5mg | 2mL | 2.5mg/mL (2500mcg/mL) | 250mcg dose = 0.1mL = 10 units. Clean math. |
| BPC-157 | 5mg | 1mL | 5mg/mL (5000mcg/mL) | 250mcg dose = 0.05mL = 5 units. Very small but precise. |
| Semaglutide | 5mg | 1mL | 5mg/mL | Small weekly doses (0.25-2.4mg). Many weeks per vial. |
| Tirzepatide | 10mg | 2mL | 5mg/mL | 2.5mg dose = 0.5mL = 50 units. |
| Tirzepatide | 30mg | 3mL | 10mg/mL | 5mg dose = 0.5mL = 50 units. Easy math. |
| CJC-1295 | 5mg | 2.5mL | 2mg/mL (2000mcg/mL) | 200mcg dose = 0.1mL = 10 units. |
| Ipamorelin | 5mg | 2.5mL | 2mg/mL (2000mcg/mL) | 200mcg dose = 0.1mL = 10 units. |
| TB-500 | 5mg | 1mL | 5mg/mL | 2.5mg dose = 0.5mL = 50 units. |

**The math users need to do at reconstitution:**
```
concentration = peptide_amount_mg / BAC_water_mL
Example: 5mg / 2mL = 2.5 mg/mL = 2500 mcg/mL
```

**Common mistakes at this stage:**
1. Shaking the vial vigorously -- destroys peptide structure, reduces potency 30-50%
2. Spraying BAC water directly onto the powder cake -- causes foaming and aggregation
3. Adding BAC water too fast -- creates foam, damages peptide
4. Reconstituting while still cold from freezer -- thermal stress damages peptide
5. Not labeling the vial -- leads to dosing errors later
6. Using sterile water instead of BAC water -- no preservative, must use within 24-48 hours vs 28-30 days
7. Reusing needles between vials -- contamination risk
8. Using too much or too little BAC water -- either can't measure doses accurately, or vial expires before using all doses

### PHASE 3: DOSING CALCULATION (The Core Math)

**The fundamental formula:**

```
Step 1: concentration_mcg_per_mL = (peptide_mg * 1000) / BAC_water_mL
Step 2: injection_volume_mL = desired_dose_mcg / concentration_mcg_per_mL
Step 3: syringe_units = injection_volume_mL * 100
```

**Worked example (BPC-157):**
```
Vial: 5mg BPC-157, reconstituted with 2mL BAC water
Desired dose: 250mcg

Step 1: (5 * 1000) / 2 = 2500 mcg/mL
Step 2: 250 / 2500 = 0.1 mL
Step 3: 0.1 * 100 = 10 units on insulin syringe
```

**Worked example (Semaglutide):**
```
Vial: 5mg semaglutide, reconstituted with 1mL BAC water
Desired dose: 0.25mg (250mcg) -- Week 1 starting dose

Step 1: (5 * 1000) / 1 = 5000 mcg/mL
Step 2: 250 / 5000 = 0.05 mL
Step 3: 0.05 * 100 = 5 units on insulin syringe
```

**Worked example (Tirzepatide):**
```
Vial: 30mg tirzepatide, reconstituted with 3mL BAC water
Desired dose: 2.5mg (2500mcg) -- Starting dose

Step 1: (30 * 1000) / 3 = 10,000 mcg/mL
Step 2: 2500 / 10000 = 0.25 mL
Step 3: 0.25 * 100 = 25 units on insulin syringe
```

**Converting mL to syringe "units":**

All standard insulin syringes are U-100 (designed for insulin at 100 units/mL).
For peptide use, "units" on the syringe are purely a VOLUME measurement:

```
100 units = 1.0 mL
50 units  = 0.5 mL
25 units  = 0.25 mL
10 units  = 0.1 mL
5 units   = 0.05 mL
1 unit    = 0.01 mL (10 microliters)
```

**Syringe sizes and when to use each:**

| Syringe | Capacity | Each Tick = | Best For | Needle |
|---------|----------|-------------|----------|--------|
| 0.3mL (30-unit) | 30 units | 1 unit (0.01mL) | Doses under 30 units. Most precise. Best for BPC-157, small GLP-1 doses. | 31G 5/16" |
| 0.5mL (50-unit) | 50 units | 1 unit (0.01mL) | Doses 30-50 units. Good middle ground. | 31G 5/16" |
| 1.0mL (100-unit) | 100 units | 2 units (0.02mL) | Larger doses. Less precise for small amounts. EACH TICK = 2 UNITS, not 1. | 29-31G 1/2" |

**CRITICAL: The 1mL syringe confusion:**
On a 1mL (100-unit) syringe, each small tick mark represents 2 units, NOT 1 unit. This is a major source of dosing errors. The 0.3mL and 0.5mL syringes have ticks at every single unit, making them much more precise.

**What info users need displayed to inject correctly:**
1. How many units to draw on their specific syringe size
2. Visual confirmation of where the plunger line should be
3. The dose in both mcg AND units
4. Which syringe size they're using (affects tick mark interpretation)

**Common dosing mistakes:**
1. Confusing mg and mcg (1000x error) -- THE most dangerous mistake
2. Misreading tick marks on 1mL syringe (each tick = 2 units, not 1)
3. Forgetting to account for how much BAC water was actually added
4. Calculating dose based on peptide name instead of checking actual vial amount
5. Not accounting for dead space in syringe needle hub (~0.01-0.03mL lost per injection)

### PHASE 4: INJECTION

**SubQ (subcutaneous) injection -- 95% of peptide injections:**

1. Clean injection site with alcohol swab, let dry
2. Pinch 1-2 inches of skin/fat
3. Insert needle at 45-90 degree angle (90 for most people with adequate subcutaneous fat)
4. Inject slowly
5. Wait 5-10 seconds before withdrawing needle
6. Apply gentle pressure with swab, do not rub

**Primary injection sites (ranked by popularity):**
1. **Abdomen** -- Most popular. 2+ inches from navel. Large surface area. Consistent absorption. Divided into 4 quadrants (upper-left, upper-right, lower-left, lower-right).
2. **Outer thigh** -- Middle third, between hip and knee. Good alternative.
3. **Upper arm** -- Back of arm, tricep area. Harder to self-inject.
4. **Upper buttock/hip** -- Love handle area. Good but less convenient.

**Rotation patterns:**
- Clock method: Rotate clockwise around navel through 4 quadrants
- Alternating: Left abdomen AM / right abdomen PM, or left thigh this week / right thigh next week
- Keep at least 1-1.5 inches between injection sites
- Don't reuse exact same spot for 5-7 days minimum
- Prevents lipohypertrophy (fatty lumps that affect absorption)

**IM (intramuscular) injection -- used for some protocols:**
- Deltoid, vastus lateralis (outer thigh), or glute
- Used more for BPC-157 near injury sites
- Requires slightly longer needle

**Time of day considerations:**

| Peptide Type | Best Time | Reason |
|-------------|-----------|--------|
| GLP-1s (semaglutide, tirzepatide) | Any time, be consistent | 7-day half-life. Some prefer AM to manage nausea during the day. Some prefer PM to sleep through side effects. |
| GH peptides (CJC/Ipa) | Before bed, fasted | Amplifies natural GH pulse during deep sleep. Must be fasted 2+ hours. |
| BPC-157 | AM and PM (if 2x daily) | Flexible. Some inject near injury site. |
| TB-500 | Any time | Long systemic action, timing less critical. |

**Fasted vs fed considerations:**
- GH-releasing peptides (CJC-1295, Ipamorelin, GHRP-6): MUST be fasted (2-3 hours no food). Carbs/fats blunt GH release.
- GLP-1s (injectable semaglutide/tirzepatide): Can be taken with or without food. No fasting required.
- BPC-157: No strict requirement. Empty stomach may slightly improve absorption.

### PHASE 5: ONGOING TRACKING

**How users currently track (ranked by popularity):**
1. Phone notes app / Apple Notes -- most common, unstructured
2. Spreadsheets (Google Sheets) -- power users, custom formulas
3. Calendar reminders -- for weekly doses like GLP-1s
4. Dedicated peptide apps (newer trend):
   - PepTracker, Peptide Tracker & Calculator, PepStackr, SHOTLOG, Regimen, MyPeptideApp, PeptidePro, PepPal
   - Most are iOS-only, launched 2025-2026
   - Features vary: dose logging, calculators, reminders, vial tracking
5. Pen and paper -- surprisingly common, especially for injection site rotation

**What users forget most:**
1. Which injection site they used last
2. When they reconstituted the vial (is it still good? 28-day limit)
3. How many doses remain in current vial
4. When to reorder before running out
5. Their exact titration schedule (especially GLP-1 users: "Am I in week 4 or week 5?")
6. Whether they already took today's dose (especially with daily protocols)

**Vial lifecycle tracking:**
```
Vial purchased -> Stored in freezer (months shelf life)
    -> Reconstituted (date recorded, 28-30 day clock starts)
    -> In use (track doses drawn, remaining volume)
    -> Empty or expired (whichever comes first)
    -> Reorder needed (ideally before previous vial runs out)
```

**Calculating doses remaining in a vial:**
```
total_doses = total_peptide_mcg / dose_per_injection_mcg
Example: 5000mcg / 250mcg = 20 doses from one vial

OR by volume:
total_volume = BAC_water_added_mL
volume_per_dose = dose_mcg / concentration_mcg_per_mL
doses_remaining = volume_remaining / volume_per_dose
```

**Vial transitions (critical pain point):**
- Users must plan ahead to avoid gaps in protocol
- New vial means new reconstitution, possibly different BAC water amount, recalculated units
- Some users reconstitute the new vial 1-2 days before the old one runs out
- With GLP-1 titration: may start new dose level with new vial
- Need to track multiple vials if stacking (e.g., BPC + TB-500)

### PHASE 6: PROTOCOL PATTERNS (Detailed Reference)

#### BPC-157 (Body Protection Compound)
- **Dose:** 250-500mcg per injection, 1-2x daily
- **Frequency:** Daily (most common: 2x/day for healing, 1x/day for maintenance)
- **Cycle:** 4-8 weeks on, 4-8 weeks off
- **Loading/maintenance variant:** Weeks 1-4 at 300mcg daily, Weeks 5-8 at 200mcg daily
- **Half-life:** ~4 minutes (extremely short, but effects are sustained through tissue signaling)
- **Vial math:** 5mg vial + 2mL BAC = 2500mcg/mL. At 250mcg 2x/day = 10 doses/day equivalent, 10 days per vial
- **Notes:** Can inject near injury site for localized effect. SubQ or IM. Also available orally (capsules).

#### Semaglutide (GLP-1 agonist, weight loss)
- **Titration schedule (Wegovy protocol):**
  - Weeks 1-4: 0.25mg/week
  - Weeks 5-8: 0.5mg/week
  - Weeks 9-12: 1.0mg/week
  - Weeks 13-16: 1.7mg/week
  - Week 17+: 2.4mg/week (maintenance)
- **Frequency:** Once weekly, same day each week
- **Half-life:** ~7 days (true once-weekly dosing)
- **Steady state:** 4-5 weeks at each dose level
- **Vial math:** 5mg vial + 1mL BAC = 5000mcg/mL. At 0.25mg/week = 5 units/week = 20 weeks per vial. At 2.4mg/week = 48 units/week = ~2 weeks per vial.
- **Dose adjustment:** If side effects are intolerable, delay escalation by 4 weeks. Can drop back one step.
- **Notes:** The LONG titration period (17+ weeks to maintenance) is a key tracking challenge.

#### Tirzepatide (GLP-1/GIP dual agonist, weight loss)
- **Titration schedule (Zepbound protocol):**
  - Weeks 1-4: 2.5mg/week
  - Weeks 5-8: 5mg/week
  - Weeks 9-12: 7.5mg/week (optional)
  - Weeks 13-16: 10mg/week (optional)
  - Weeks 17-20: 12.5mg/week (optional)
  - Week 21+: 15mg/week (max)
- **Maintenance doses:** 5mg, 10mg, or 15mg weekly (patient/provider choice)
- **Frequency:** Once weekly
- **Half-life:** ~5 days
- **Dose increments:** Always 2.5mg steps, minimum 4 weeks between increases
- **Vial math:** 30mg vial + 3mL BAC = 10mg/mL. At 2.5mg/week = 25 units = 12 weeks per vial. At 10mg/week = 100 units = 3 weeks per vial.
- **Notes:** Time to max dose can be ~21 weeks (5 months). Many users settle at 5-10mg.

#### Retatrutide (Triple agonist: GLP-1/GIP/Glucagon)
- **Titration schedule (from clinical trials):**
  - Weeks 1-4: 1mg/week (some start at 2mg)
  - Weeks 5-8: 2mg/week (or 4mg)
  - Weeks 9-12: 4mg/week (or 8mg)
  - Week 13+: 8-12mg/week (maintenance)
- **Frequency:** Once weekly
- **Dose increments:** Doubles each step (more aggressive than semaglutide/tirzepatide)
- **Notes:** Triple receptor agonist means more GI side effects possible. Gradual titration is even more critical -- skipping steps nearly doubles side effect rates. Not yet FDA approved (as of March 2026).

#### CJC-1295 (no DAC) + Ipamorelin (GH stack)
- **Dose:** 100-300mcg of each peptide per injection
- **Standard protocol:** 200mcg CJC + 200mcg Ipamorelin, before bed
- **Frequency:** Daily, 5 days on / 2 days off (weekdays on, weekends off)
- **Cycle:** 8-12 weeks on, 4 weeks off
- **Half-life:** CJC-1295 no DAC ~30 min, Ipamorelin ~2 hours
- **Timing:** Must be fasted 2+ hours. Before bed is ideal (amplifies natural sleep GH pulse).
- **Notes:** Often drawn from 2 separate vials into 1 syringe (draw Ipa first, then CJC). Some vendors sell pre-blended vials.

#### TB-500 (Thymosin Beta-4, healing)
- **Loading phase (weeks 1-4 to 6):**
  - 2-2.5mg, 2x per week (Mon/Thu typical)
  - Total: 4-5mg per week during loading
- **Maintenance phase (weeks 5-10+):**
  - 2mg, 1x per week
- **Full cycle:** 10-12 weeks total, then 4-week washout
- **Half-life:** Longer systemic action than BPC-157
- **Notes:** Often stacked with BPC-157. BPC daily + TB-500 2x/week is a common healing stack.

#### Common Stacking Patterns:
- **Healing stack:** BPC-157 250mcg 2x/day + TB-500 2.5mg 2x/week. 8-12 week cycle.
- **Weight loss:** Semaglutide OR Tirzepatide weekly (not both).
- **Anti-aging GH:** CJC+Ipa nightly 5/2 schedule. Often combined with a healing peptide.
- **Triple stack (advanced):** BPC + TB-500 + CJC/Ipa. Multiple vials, complex schedule.

### PHASE 7: STORAGE AND SHELF LIFE

**Before reconstitution (lyophilized powder):**
- Room temperature: stable for weeks to months
- Refrigerator (2-8C): months to a year+
- Freezer (-20C): years of stability
- Key enemies: moisture, heat, light, repeated freeze-thaw cycles

**After reconstitution (in solution):**
- With BAC water: 28-30 days refrigerated (2-8C)
- With sterile water (no preservative): 24-48 hours only
- NEVER leave reconstituted peptides at room temperature
- Keep in refrigerator door or dedicated box, away from light
- Do NOT freeze reconstituted solution (damages peptide structure)

**Practical implications for app:**
- User needs to log reconstitution date
- App should warn when approaching 28-day expiry
- If vial has doses remaining but hits 28 days = must discard
- This creates tension between BAC water amount (more water = more volume per dose = may not finish in time)

### KEY INSIGHTS FOR APP DESIGN

**The 5 critical calculations every user must perform:**
1. How much BAC water to add (sets concentration)
2. What is my concentration (mcg/mL)
3. How many units to draw for my dose
4. How many doses left in this vial
5. When does this vial expire

**The 5 things users track ongoing:**
1. Did I take today's dose (yes/no, with undo)
2. Where did I inject (site rotation)
3. How many doses remain in vial
4. What phase/week of titration am I in (GLP-1s)
5. When to reorder

**Unit confusion is the #1 danger:**
- Vials labeled in mg, protocols often in mcg
- 1 mg = 1,000 mcg (the 1000x error risk)
- Syringe "units" are volume, not drug units
- IU (International Units) are a different system entirely (for HGH, HCG)
- Users mix up "10 units on syringe" with "10mg of peptide"

**The ideal app flow mirrors the real workflow:**
1. Add peptide (name, vial size in mg)
2. Reconstitute (enter BAC water amount -> app calculates concentration)
3. Set protocol (dose amount, frequency, cycle length, titration schedule if applicable)
4. Daily: one-tap dose logging -> app shows exact syringe units, suggests injection site, counts down remaining doses
5. Vial management: shows days until expiry, doses remaining, auto-prompts reorder

## UX BEST PRACTICES (Health App Research)

### Onboarding (3-5 steps max)
1. Value screen (no sign-up required)
2. First calculation (immediate value BEFORE account creation)
3. Personalization (what peptides do you use?)
4. Account creation (optional, framed as "Save your protocols")
- Anti-pattern: NEVER force account creation before first use
- Anti-pattern: NEVER ask notification permission on first screen

### Dashboard — "Today View" Architecture
```
[Header: Today's Date + Streak Counter]
[HERO: Next Dose Card — large, one-tap actionable]
  - Peptide name + dose amount
  - Visual syringe marking showing draw amount
  - "Log Dose" CTA button
  - Countdown to next dose
[Secondary: Today's remaining doses]
[Tertiary: Supply status — progress bars per vial]
[Bottom Nav: Home | Calculator | Protocols | History]
```

### One-Tap Dose Logging (CRITICAL UX)
- From notification: Tap → See dose → One tap "Logged" (or Skip/Snooze)
- From dashboard: Next dose card has prominent "Log Dose"
- Snooze options: 10min, 30min, 1hr
- Primary "Log" button within thumb reach (bottom 40% of screen)
- Haptic feedback on confirmation

### Calculator UX
- Hybrid input: stepper buttons + direct keypad tap
- Real-time calculation (no "Calculate" button needed)
- Visual syringe display showing exact draw mark
- Support multiple syringe sizes (U-100, U-50, U-30)
- Show the math transparently: "5mg / 2mL = 2.5mg/mL, 250mcg = 0.1mL = 10 units"
- Builds trust and lets users verify

### Inventory/Supply UX
- Progress bar per vial: Green >50%, Yellow 25-50%, Red <25%
- Low supply push notification at configurable threshold
- Reconstitution date tracking + days until 28-day expiry
- Multiple vials of same peptide tracked independently
- Visual vial graphic that "empties" as doses are logged

### Visual Design
- Dark mode default (users dose morning/evening)
- Background: Dark gray #121212-#1E1E1E, NOT pure black
- Text: Dimmed white #E0E0E0, NOT pure white
- Accent: Teal/cyan #00BCD4 — precision, science, trust
- Success: Muted green / Warning: Amber / Error: Soft red
- Minimum 16px body text, WCAG 4.5:1 contrast
- Micro-animations for positive reinforcement

### PWA-Specific Patterns
- Don't show install prompt immediately — wait until after first calculation
- Cache calculator for full offline use via service workers
- Soft opt-in for notifications after value experienced
- Guide iOS users through "Add to Home Screen" (no auto-install on iOS)

### Trust & Credibility
- Show the formula/math behind every calculation
- "Your data stays on your device" messaging
- Medical disclaimer visible but not intrusive
- No ads — signals premium quality
- Version the calculation engine, show "last updated"

### Streak Tracking — With Flexibility
- Cumulative tracking (total doses logged) not just consecutive
- Recovery mechanics (freeze/rest day preserves streak)
